Our Core

CrossNet Global exists to glorify God among the nations,

whether overseas or in the USA. We are a multi-level resource for just about anything related to cross-cultural Christian missions. We are staffed by experienced, senior level missions personnel bringing decades of management and training experience plus fourteen post-graduate degrees. Our focus is on five specific endeavors: A. Providing Resources, Training and Mentoring for: 1. Churches that have sent, or wish to send, cross-cultural missionaries 2. Mission organizations that may be limited in training, resourcing or supporting field personnel 3. Christian Universities that offer training including BA or MA programs in missions B. Providing suggestions for unreached people-groups for cross-cultural missions C. Providing tools for churches to minister to refugees and immigrants God is bringing to the United States.

Partnerships

The following organizations are TEAM ASSOCIATES which have partnered with CrossNet. Each organization brings a professional service to the CrossNet family. While CrossNet does not charge for its services, some of the following may charge for their services. Each organization is top-flight and CrossNet recommends them for your organization’s needs.


A. Telios Teaches – Child Safety Training

This organization specializes in child-protection training. In today’s internet-saturated, pornography-soaked culture, child sex abuse prevention has become a driving concern among churches and mission organizations. Indeed, sex abuse has closed both churches and missions organization. Telios Teaches www.teliosteaches.com is a national leader in providing Child Safety Training and Anti-Harassment Training for churches and missions organizations. They are a subset of Telios Law www.telioslaw.com, which provides legal resources for churches and missions organizations. Because of the relationship between Telios Teaches and CrossNet, those using Telios Teaches training can obtain a substantial discount for their services. Just contact CrossNet at CrossNet Email for more information. CrossNet is pleased to partner with Telios Teaches, and we welcome them to our family of resources.

B. Salt and Light Group – Field Security Training

Salt and Light Group provides training and risk consultation for those involved in global missions. The Basic Course is a full immersion live environment experience and prepares students for the realities they will face when going into missions work. Students will live, learn and communicate in a simulated missions context. The Advanced Course prepares students for the realities of high persecution, high risk, and conflict zones with emphasis on creative access, communication threats, and physical risk reduction. Courses are taught by experienced trainers in mission work, military special operations, and global risk management. Specific project consultation is available by request and is tailored to your unique situation. Learn more at www.saltandlightgroup.org/training CrossNet is pleased to partner with Salt and Light Group, and we welcome them to our family of resources.

C. Institute for Community Transformation – Online BA and MA Education

The Institute for Community Transformation (ICT) provides an innovative, integrative, and experiential college-level education that emphasizes whole-person formation for the purpose of empowering leaders to catalyze whole-community transformation around the world. They help students to pursue God’s presence and become deeply rooted in God’s truth while being prepared for a vocation. They nurture spiritual growth and Christlikeness while gaining competencies in systems thinking and real-world problem solving. ICT is linked to two universities (Kairos University and City University) that share Christ-centered faith, and focus on competency-based education. CrossNet is pleased to partner with ICT, and we welcome them to our family of resources.

I. Sending Organization Recommendations

While there are scores of sending agencies, the following ten are organizations with which CrossNet is familiar...

While there are scores of sending agencies, the following ten are organizations with which CrossNet is familiar and comfortable recommending. Each of the following brings unique elements to the global missions conversation. (CrossNet has no financial connection with any of these organizations.)

  • YWAM
    Youth With A Mission. A global movement, full of young people driven by a passion to know God and make Him known. Excellent hands on DTS training, variety of ministries, much flexibility.
  • IMB
    International Mission Board. If one is Southern Baptist this is an excellent choice.  Full financial support, much training, good accountability, excellent teaming and supervision.
  • Frontiers
    “To inspire transformational movements to Christ within least-reached Muslim people groups by sending teams to catalyze change.” If one is feeling led toward Muslims this would be a good choice.
  • East-West
     Especially good for those who have a desire to evangelize and plant multiplying churches in unreached people groups.  The mission of East-West is to mobilize the Body of Christ to evangelize the lost and equip local believers to multiply disciples and churches among the unreached.
  • Pioneers
    “Empowring gospel-riven Christians to go to the ends of the earth.” For more than 40 years, Pioneers missionaries have been using innovative and creative means to bring the gospel to every corner of the globe.
  • Cru
    (formerly Campus Crusade for Christ) is a caring community passionate about connecting people to Jesus Christ. Their purpose is helping to fulfill the Great Commission in the power of the Holy Spirit by winning people to faith in Jesus Christ, building them in their faith and sending them to win and build others, doing evangelism and discipleship in a variety of creative ways.
  • TEAM
    is a global missions agency that partners with local churches to send missionaries and plant reproducing churches around the world. TEAM missionaries serve through church planting, community development, healthcare, social justice, business as mission and more. But whatever their ministry, each missionary has the same goal: proclaiming the Gospel in word and deed.
  • SEND
    International sends missionaries to Asia, Europe, Americas, and Eurasia, focusing on missionaries that live out the gospel in front of their neighbors. To do this, they learn the language well and live among the people they hope to reach. They work in field teams. Some teams work very closely together on the same project and ministry; other teams work more independently but check in with each other regularly.
  • Operation Mobilization
    “To see vibrant communities of Jesus followers among the least reached.” OM was founded to mobilize young people to live and share the Gospel of Jesus, to transform the lives of those living in both physical and spiritual poverty, sharing a holistic gospel with the people we serve. OM started in Mexico and had spread to Europe and India by 1963.
  • Go To Nations
    A missionary organization fully devoted to equipping, training, discipling, raising up and sending our missionaries to complete the Great Commission.

IV. FIELD SECURITY TRAINING

Everyone involved in missions these days understands that security has become a critical issue.


Everyone involved in missions these days understands that security has become a critical issue.

  • Whether it involves kidnap avoidance or survival training, safety in travel, internet security or global terrorism, the mission field has become hazardous deployment for many. It has been said that the “low hanging fruit” in missions has been gleaned; what remains now are the hard-to-reach and sometimes downright hostile places. That being said, field security training is increasingly a must.

  • Stateside organizations have sprung up to train people going to the field, whether for short-term or long-term deployment. All sending organizations including churches should have relationships with at least one of the following security training organizations, for which we use numbers rather than providing written identification below:

  • 1, 2 – The first two orgs are well established with seasoned professionals. One of these provides realistic kidnap and hostage training. Both provide several classroom trainings annually for numerous security related field issues.

  • 3 – The third security org is headed by former US special operations personnel, focuses on how to maintain a low profile, and provides on-site training, globally. This org also provides realistic situations as training tools.

  • 4 – The fourth organization is composed of retired law enforcement, retired US armed forces personnel, missionaries who have survived kidnap and hostage, and senior mission org personnel who create and implement security policies within their orgs.

Why CrossNet Global ?

The last command Christ gave

before returning to the Father, was to “Go and make disciples of all nations….” Most Christians have the belief but feel they lack the capacity to obey this command. Even many churches and mission organizations have good intentions but lack basic essentials in preparing and supporting field candidates.

There is a have-versus-have-not gap
in many organizations’ ability to prepare, equip and support missionaries they send, or may wish to send. Large churches and mission organizations are often blessed with professional staff providing excellent training and ongoing support.

However:

Smaller “mom and pop” mission organizations often don’t have the professional staffing they need to accomplish many essential tasks of preparation and ongoing care for their missionaries.

Smaller churches which send (or wish to send) missionaries are usually in the same boat, lacking professional staff, training, and caring resources for their field staff.

Christian Universities with mission training programs may have good staff and training capabilities, but unless they have several mission departmental faculty, it’s unlikely they have the depth and breadth of knowledge, tools and experience CrossNet offers. Furthermore, universities don’t provide ongoing member care training and support.

Connectivity with other like-minded organizations is often lacking, and even when provided by denominations, it is sometimes actually limited by denominational boundaries, leadership, traditions, theology, or staffing policies.

5. Of the few Stateside organizations offering similar training:

a. Most don’t have access to the depth and breadth of knowledge, training and experience CrossNet offers

b. Few are committed to working with smaller churches and sending organizations

c. Few are intentionally engaged with credible academic institutions

d. Many are restricted to interfacing within denominational parameters

e. And few offer their services free of charge.

CrossNet answers this deficit with a depth of missions and professional experience and skillsets, leadership experience, academic horsepower and passion.

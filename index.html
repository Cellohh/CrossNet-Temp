About

Executive Summary

Field preparation – field training modules taught by Executive Level trainers with decades of field experience. Pre-Field Psychological Screening provided by Ph.D. level psychologist. Member Care – modules for missionary preparation and sending organizations. Short-Term Missions coaching and leadership provided by experienced field missionaries. Counseling for active field staff (max. 6 sessions). Strategic planning for churches wishing to send field staff. ESL training for churches wishing to minister to immigrants/refugees Stateside. Training in use of WhatsApp and Zoom to reach people in 10/40 window from their own living rooms.

Our Core

CrossNet Global exists to glorify God among the nations,

whether overseas or in the USA. We are a resource for anything related to cross-cultural Christian missions. We are staffed by experienced, senior-level missions personnel bringing decades of management and training experience plus fourteen post-graduate degrees.
Our focus is on six specific endeavors:
A. Providing Resources, Training and Mentoring for:
1. Churches that have sent, or wish to send, cross-cultural missionaries.
2. Mission organizations that may be limited in training, resourcing or supporting field personnel.
3. Christian academic and training organizations that offer training including BA or MA programs in missions.
4. Training in ESL so churches may reach immigrants/refugees whom God is bringing to the USA.
5. Training in use of online communication to reach around the globe from one’s own kitchen.
6. Providing training for reaching refugees and immigrants in one’s own community.

Faith Orientation

We are a Christian, evangelical, non-denominational consortium. Our faith-definitional documents include a) The Apostles’ Creed, and b) The Lausanne Covenant.

Partnerships

"I will make you as a light for the nations, that my salvation may reach to the end of the earth." (Isa. 49:6b)

The following organizations are TEAM ASSOCIATES which have partnered with CrossNet. Each organization brings a professional service to the CrossNet family. While CrossNet does not charge for its services, some of the following may charge for their services. Each organization is top-flight and CrossNet recommends them for your organization’s needs.


A. Telios Teaches – Child Safety Training

This organization specializes in child-protection training. In today’s internet-saturated, pornography-soaked culture, child sex abuse prevention has become a driving concern among churches and mission organizations. Indeed, sex abuse has closed both churches and missions organization. Telios Teaches www.teliosteaches.com is a national leader in providing Child Safety Training and Anti-Harassment Training for churches and missions organizations. They are a subset of Telios Law www.telioslaw.com, which provides legal resources for churches and missions organizations. Because of the relationship between Telios Teaches and CrossNet, those using Telios Teaches training can obtain a substantial discount for their services. Just contact CrossNet at info@crossnetglobal.com for more information. CrossNet is pleased to partner with Telios Teaches, and we welcome them to our family of resources.

B. Salt and Light Group – Field Security Training

Salt and Light Group provides training and risk consultation for those involved in global missions. The Basic Course is a full immersion live environment experience and prepares students for the realities they will face when going into missions work. Students will live, learn and communicate in a simulated missions context. The Advanced Course prepares students for the realities of high persecution, high risk, and conflict zones with emphasis on creative access, communication threats, and physical risk reduction. Courses are taught by experienced trainers in mission work, military special operations, and global risk management. Specific project consultation is available by request and is tailored to your unique situation. Learn more at www.saltandlightgroup.org/training CrossNet is pleased to partner with Salt and Light Group, and we welcome them to our family of resources.

C. Institute for Community Transformation – Online BA and MA Education

The Institute for Community Transformation (ICT) provides an innovative, integrative, and experiential college-level education that emphasizes whole-person formation for the purpose of empowering leaders to catalyze whole-community transformation around the world. They help students to pursue God’s presence and become deeply rooted in God’s truth while being prepared for a vocation. They nurture spiritual growth and Christlikeness while gaining competencies in systems thinking and real-world problem solving. ICT is linked to two universities (Kairos University and City University) that share Christ-centered faith, and focus on competency-based education. CrossNet is pleased to partner with ICT, and we welcome them to our family of resources.

II. FIELD SECURITY TRAINING

Everyone involved in missions these days...



Everyone involved in missions these days understands that security has become a critical issue.

  • Whether it involves kidnap avoidance or survival training, safety in travel, internet security or global terrorism, the mission field has become hazardous deployment for many. It has been said that the “low hanging fruit” in missions has been gleaned; what remains now are the hard-to-reach and sometimes downright hostile places. That being said, field security training is increasingly a must.

  • Stateside organizations have sprung up to train people going to the field, whether for short-term or long-term deployment. All sending organizations including churches should have relationships with at least one of the following security training organizations, for which we use numbers rather than providing written identification below:

  • 1, 2 – The first two orgs are well established with seasoned professionals. One of these provides realistic kidnap and hostage training. Both provide several classroom trainings annually for numerous security related field issues.

  • 3 – The third security org is headed by former US special operations personnel, focuses on how to maintain a low profile, and provides on-site training, globally. This org also provides realistic situations as training tools.

  • 4 – The fourth organization is composed of retired law enforcement, retired US armed forces personnel, missionaries who have survived kidnap and hostage, and senior mission org personnel who create and implement security policies within their orgs.

  • For contact information for any of these orgs, contact us at info@crossnetglobal.com

Why CrossNet Global?

The last command Christ gave

before returning to the Father, was to “Go and make disciples of all nations….” Most Christians have the belief but feel they lack the capacity to obey this command. Even many churches and mission organizations have good intentions but lack basic essentials in preparing and supporting field candidates.

There is a have-versus-have-not gap
in many organizations’ ability to prepare, equip and support missionaries they send, or may wish to send. Large churches and mission organizations are often blessed with professional staff providing excellent training and ongoing support.

However:

Some mission organizations often don’t have the professional staffing they need to accomplish many essential tasks of preparation and ongoing care for their missionaries.

Some churches which send (or wish to send) missionaries are sometimes in the same boat, lacking professional staff, training and caring resources for their field staff.

Christian Universities, Colleges, Bible schools or other training organizations with mission training programs may have good staff and training capabilities, but unless they have several mission departmental faculty, it’s unlikely they have the depth and breadth of knowledge, tools and experience CrossNet offers. Furthermore, universities don’t provide ongoing member care training and support.

Connectivity with other like-minded organizations is often lacking, and even when provided by denominations, it is sometimes actually limited by denominational boundaries, leadership, traditions, theology, or staffing policies.

While there are one or two other Stateside organizations offering similar training CrossNet is unique because:

a. We provide a depth of academic training, practical knowledge, field training and skillsets born of decades of experience

b. We are committed to working with all sizes of churches and sending organizations

c. We are attached to a credible academic institution for training

d. We work cross-denominationally

e. And we operate online, therefore are free of charge

CrossNet enters this discussion with a depth of missions and professional experience and skillsets, leadership experience, academic horsepower and passion.
